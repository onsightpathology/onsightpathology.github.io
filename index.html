The Adoption Barrier

Artificial intelligence holds immense potential to enhance objectivity and efficiency in histopathology. Yet, its integration into clinical practice is critically stalled. Pathologists face a fragmented landscape of proprietary WSI formats, incompatible viewers, and data security hurdles that make deploying AI tools a complex, expensive, and often prohibitive endeavor. The best AI model is useless if it can't be used.

A Radically Simple Approach

Vendor-Agnostic

Works with any slide viewer on any OS. If it's on your screen, OnSight can analyze it. No APIs, no plugins needed.

100% Secure & Private

All processing happens locally on your machine. Patient data and proprietary images are never transmitted or uploaded.

Real-Time Feedback

Leverages local GPU/CPU power to deliver live AI inferences as you navigate slides, enabling an interactive workflow.

Example Use Cases

OnSight comes with a suite of pre-trained models to assist with common histopathological tasks. Users can easily switch between models to fit their needs.

Brain Tumor Classification

Utilizes a powerful Vision Transformer (ViT) to provide real-time classification of common brain tumor patterns (glial, meningiothelial, etc.) on H&E slides, guiding initial diagnosis.

Automated Mitosis Counting

Objectify tumor grading by automatically detecting and counting mitotic figures in real-time. Calibrate results to physical area (mm²) for reproducible, report-ready metrics.

IHC Quantification

A YOLO-based model segments and counts Ki-67 positive and negative nuclei, providing a rapid and objective proliferation index. Includes controls for overlay transparency to aid interpretation.

Interactive AI Chat

Integrates a Vision-Language Model that supports natural-language queries about the current image. Ask questions like, "What feature makes this glial?" to get deeper, interpretable insights from the AI.

OnSight-WSI Extension

OnSight-WSI is a dedicated Whole Slide Image extension built natively for Windows GPUs. It processes full-resolution slides (SVS, NDPI, TIFF) through a custom interactive viewer, enabling real-time, aligned overlays of HAVOC morphologic heterogeneity and RetinaNet-based mitosis detection. Common Questions

What are the system requirements?

For optimal real-time performance, a Windows PC with a dedicated NVIDIA GPU (RTX 20-series or newer) is recommended. The software remains functional on CPU-only systems with a slight delay in inference. For macOS, system with both Apple Silicon and Intel processor are supported, although computational heavy module may experience slower performance for Intel processor.

Can I use my own AI models?

The current version is distributed with the pre-packaged, validated models. We are actively working on a framework to allow users to easily integrate their own custom models in future releases.

What is the current status of OnSight regarding clinical diagnostic use?

OnSight is an experimental and research tool that is intended to support future clinical applications but has not yet been validated for clinical diagnostic use. At this stage, it is provided strictly for research, educational, and investigational purposes.
